💡 语言点 Language Point · 您 vs 你
您 vs 你 — Two ways to say "you"
Same meaning, different formality levels.
nǐ
你
Casual / informal
Friends, peers, classmates
VS
nín
您
Formal / respectful
Teachers, elders, strangers
老师，您好！Hello, teacher! (respectful ✓)
朋友，你好！Hello, friend! (casual ✓)
💡 语言点 Language Point · Plural Suffix 们
们 — Making Words Plural
Add 们 after pronouns or nouns for people to make them plural.
nǐ → nǐmen
你 → 你们
you → you (all)
tā → tāmen
他 → 他们
he → they
tóngxué → men
同学 → 同学们
classmate → classmates
⚠️Note: 们 is only used with people. 学生们 ✓  |  书们 ✗

🌏 文化拓展 Culture · Chinese Name Structure
中国人的姓名 · Chinese Names
中国人名字的顺序是：姓 + 名 (Family name first, then given name)
姓 Surname
李
Family name · comes first
+
名 Given name
文
Personal name · comes after
李文
姓李 + 名文
Lǐ Wén
陈天中
姓陈 + 名天中
Chén Tiānzhōng
白家月
姓白 + 名家月
Bái Jiāyuè
💡In English contexts, Chinese people often reverse the order: Li Wen → Wen Li. In Chinese, always write the surname first.

Lesson 1
Mā zhòng má, wǒ fàng mǎ.
妈种麻，我放马。
Mom plants flax. I tend the horse.
Mǎ chī má, māma mà mǎ.
马吃麻，妈骂马。

Lesson 2
Qī jiā yī, zài jiǎn yī, jiāwán jiǎnwán děngyú jǐ?
七加一，再减一，加完减完等于几？
Jiāwán jiǎnwán hái shì qī.
加完减完还是七。
Seven plus one, minus one again. Still just seven!
🎋 文化拓展 Culture Corner — Greetings & Etiquette
🌏 Chinese Communication Etiquette
🤝 Handshakes
Common in formal settings. A brief, light handshake is standard — not too firm.
🙏 Nodding
A slight nod shows respect — very common in everyday greetings and acknowledgment.
📛 Use Titles
Address people as title + surname: 王老师, 陈先生. Using only a first name can seem rude.
👴 Elders First
Greet the oldest person in the group first. It shows respect for seniority in Chinese culture.
