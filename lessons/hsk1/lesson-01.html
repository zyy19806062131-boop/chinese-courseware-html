💡 语言点 Language Point · Honorific 您
您 vs 你 — Two ways to say "you"
Both mean "you", but they carry different levels of formality.
nǐ
你
Casual / informal
Friends, classmates, peers
VS
nín
您
Formal / respectful
Teachers, elders, strangers
老师，您好！Hello, teacher! (respectful ✓)
朋友，你好！Hello, friend! (casual ✓)

💡 语言点 Language Point · Plural Suffix 们
们 — Making Words Plural
Add 们 (men) after pronouns or nouns for people to make them plural.
nǐ → nǐmen
你 → 你们
you → you (plural)
tā → tāmen
他 → 他们
he → they
tóngxué → tóngxuémen
同学 → 同学们
classmate → classmates
⚠️ Note: 们 is only used with people. You can say 学生们 (students) but not 书们 for books.

🌏 文化拓展 Culture Corner
🎋 Chinese Greeting Etiquette
🤝 Handshakes
Common in formal settings. A brief, light handshake is the norm — not too firm.
🙏 Nodding
A slight nod shows respect and acknowledgment — very common in everyday greetings.
👴 Elders First
Always greet the oldest person in the group first — it shows respect for seniority.
📛 Use Titles
Address people as title + surname: 王老师, 李经理. Using first names alone can seem rude.
